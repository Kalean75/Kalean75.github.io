Course Reflection

Overall, I found this course to be quite enjoyable, as it allowed my to blend my love of art and design with my love of programming

Javascript I found to be the easiest part to learn, as I already had a background in both Python and Java, so most of the concepts tended to be the same

Similarly, HTML itself is fairly easy and straightforward, with the only real requirements being remembering tags, and having a good sense of layout

CSS however, was a huge pain for me, stuff like floats and wrapping and other concepts were extremely confusing for me at first, oftentimes resulting in total breaking of the page layout

However, once I started to get more proficient at it, it became amazing, as you can manipulate a whole lot with it, and it allows for one to use their aesthetic eye to open a world of near infinite possibilities

While I've tended to be more of a "back end" guy, I feel that knowing at least the basics of web-design is a very important skill to be familiar with, and this course more then delivered on that front

Bootstrap in particular is an extremely handy tool to know, and it's nice to be able to delv into such a thing in an intro level course
